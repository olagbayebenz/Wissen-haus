Research Intern

TypeInternship
Duration3-6 months
LocationRemote/Hybrid

About This Internship

Assist with policy research and data behind papers like our Employability Pipeline series. You'll conduct research that informs how we measure impact and advocate for policy changes that benefit Nigerian youth.

What You'll Do

  • Conduct literature reviews and desktop research on education/employment topics
  • Assist with data collection and analysis for research projects
  • Support the development of policy briefs and research papers
  • Help create infographics and data visualizations
  • Coordinate surveys and collect feedback from participants
  • Research best practices from other nonprofits and international organizations

What You'll Learn

  • Research methodology and data analysis
  • Policy research and advocacy
  • Data visualization and communication
  • How data drives nonprofit decision-making
  • Education policy landscape in Nigeria

Who We're Looking For

  • Currently enrolled student or recent graduate in social sciences, economics, education, or related field
  • Strong research and analytical skills
  • Proficiency with Excel, Google Sheets, and basic data analysis
  • Excellent written and communication skills
  • Ability to work independently and manage time effectively
  • Curiosity about education systems and youth employment

Benefits

  • Experience conducting research that informs real policy decisions
  • Mentorship from policy and research experts
  • Potential to be co-authored on published research papers
  • Certificate and strong LinkedIn recommendation
  • Flexible remote/hybrid work arrangement
