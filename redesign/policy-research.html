Beyond Unemployment

Fixing Nigeria's Education-to-Employment Transition, five policies that could transform youth employability in Nigeria.

SDG 4 · Quality Education SDG 8 · Decent Work
Prepared by Benz Olagbaye, Founder & Executive Director, Wissen-Haus Youth Empowerment Foundation.
Download PDF 11 pages · PDF

Nigeria does not have a youth unemployment crisis alone. It has an education-to-employment transition crisis, a pipeline that leaks at every stage.

Youth unemployment sat at 6.5% in late 2025, informal employment at 93% of all jobs, and 14.4% of young Nigerians were neither in education, employment nor training. These are symptoms. The paper introduces the Wissen-Haus Employability Pipeline:

01 Education→ 02 Skills→ 03 Experience→ 04 Networks→ 05 Employment

Five recommendations

1Embed employability skills in tertiary curriculaFed. Ministry of Education / NUC · 2 yearsEducation
2Institutionalise mandatory work-based learningNUC / NBTE · 3 years (phased)Experience
3Scale a national digital & future-skills programmeMin. of Comms & Digital Economy / NITDA · to 2027Skills
4Build a national career guidance & LMI systemFed. Ministry of Education / NBS · 2 yearsNetworks
5Introduce an employer incentive frameworkFed. Ministry of Finance / FIRS · 3 yearsEmployment
