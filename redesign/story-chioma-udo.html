The Journey

I grew up believing that opportunities like Oxford scholarships were for people like me. My family had limited resources. I didn't have the connections or the money for special coaching. How could I possibly compete nationally or internationally?

Wissen-Haus Trade Fair changed my mindset. More importantly, mentors at Wissen-Haus believed in me. One mentor, herself an Oxford graduate, spent months helping me prepare my scholarship applications. She taught me that background doesn't determine potential. She helped me craft a compelling narrative around my challenges and how I'd overcome them.

I applied to multiple scholarships locally and internationally. The rejections stung, but mentors kept encouraging me. When I was accepted to Oxford with a full scholarship, I couldn't believe it. I remember calling my mentor in tears—happy tears.

Now, in my final year at Oxford studying AI, I'm already thinking about my next chapter. I want to return to Nigeria and work on AI solutions for African problems. The confidence Wissen-Haus gave me made all the difference. I learned that belief comes before achievement.

Advice to Scholarship Seekers

"Your background is your story, not your ceiling. Scholarships exist for people like you. Be honest about your journey. Get mentorship—don't try to do it alone. And believe in yourself before anyone else does. That belief is what will carry you through the hard times."
